Introduction

Richmond is the capital of the beautiful state of Virginia. It was also my home for the first 18 years of my life. Moving away from home has certainly helped grow my appreciation for the wonderful city. I hope to inspire the love I have for Richmond, Virginia in others.

Food

While Richmond has not always thrived in the culinary aspect, it has raised its bar in recent years. I will share two of my favotire restaurants in Richmond.

Blue Atlas This restaurant is known for its seasonally rotating menu. It features dishes from around the world. It also has a market with pastries and drink specials.web

Rappahannock This restaurant is famous for having the best oysters in town and has a farm-to-table menu.

History

One of Richmond's most wonderful qualities is the history that it holds-- some of which being controversial, however, important nonetheless. Below outlines some major historical events as well as historical sites that can be visited

Historical Events

1861

Richmond becomes the capital of the Confederate States of America .

  • During the civil war, Richmond played an important role for the Confedaracy
  • Richmond was able to transform from an agricultural to industrial town during the course of the civil war

Bread Riot

1863

Hungry women sought for change

In 1863, inflation caused a financial burden for families simply trying to buy food. Women decided to talk to the VA governor at the time but he refused. The conflict led to attacks and, eventually, military involvement.

Post World War II Growth

1946

Richmond became the fastest growing Industrial Center in the United States

The economy was able to grow because the record level of business activity taking place in the city.

Historical Sites

Hollywood Cemetery

The Hollywood Cemetery is the burial site for many historical figures including:

  • James Monroe
  • John Tyler
  • Jefferson Davis

Virginia State Capitol

  • The oldest elected legislative body of North America is housed here
  • Construction was completed in 1788
  • It is now a National Historic Landmark

James River

One of my favorite things about Richmond is its access to the James River. There are so many activities and memories that I have associated with the James.

Boating

The James is such a fun place to do water sports or tube behind a boat!

Rock Hopping

Belle Isle is a wonderful destination in Richmond for people who want to walk along the river or walk on the rocks.

Fishing off the dock

I have so many fond memories of making homeade fishing rods out of sticks and fishing off of a dock into the James River.
